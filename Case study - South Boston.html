According to the above research, we can already know that South Boston is an area of student population with relatively average family living density. Next, we obtained Land use types of the community through Parcel data, including the number of residential households corresponding to each Parcel, Own occ, floor Numbers and other relevant data, and explored the correlation between them by combining the previously obtained population density data. We also introduce the concept of BCR as another way to calculate urban density and compare it with population density.

Census mapping shows that the overall number of students in this community is relatively small compared with Allston, but the overall student living density distribution is relatively uniform, and the relatively high-density student living area is scattered in this area. This indicates that there is no obvious distinction between students and families living in this area. In general, the distribution of the student population is relatively unaffected by the absence of directly adjacent schools or public transport stations in the neighborhood.

Through parcel mapping research, it is found that the high-density residential areas on the west side of the community are relatively few and scattered. However, in the overall spatial pattern, the proportion of open space and residence in this area is balanced, and the type of residence is mainly townhouse, which is a relatively suitable area to live in.

To get a more accurate understanding of the rental situation in this region, we imported the total number of households of all types of housing, housing type, whether it is a lease, whether it is a residential land for comparative analysis. Through the diagram, we find that residential condo is the largest number of households in this community, followed by residential three family and residential two family.

These three types of residences are partly rented and partly self-used. On the whole, the proportion of renting and self-use in this area is half open, so it can be inferred that the residential stability of the community is relatively good.
